02 — Why It's Happening: Reason 1
Short-plan customers renew better per event — but hit renewal far more often
All new installs are PayG. Customers choose their plan duration — 28 days or shorter (7-day, 14-day). 28-day customers renew at 85%; short-plan customers renew at 90%. But a 7-day customer hits their renewal window 4× more often per month. More renewal events at the same rate means more absolute losses per week.

03 — Why It's Happening: Reason 2
Recoveries — customers who come back after churning — dropped sharply in April
Net drain = Gross Churn − Installs − Recoveries. Recoveries are customers who missed the 15-day R15 window but recharged later. They had been improving through March (1,400–1,500/week) and were a meaningful buffer. In April they dropped back to ~1,050/week — adding ~400 extra net drain per week on top of the pool effect.

05 — So What
What this means — and where it goes from here
Open Question — Why Are Recoveries Dropping?
Recoveries fell from ~1,500/week in March to ~1,050/week in April — contributing ~400 extra net drain/week
Hypothesis: As the mix of churned customers shifts toward PayG, recoveries may naturally be lower. A 28D customer who churns paid ₹565 — they're more likely to return. A 1-day customer who churns paid ₹23. Lower commitment may mean lower win-back rate. This needs verification — compare recovery rate for PayG churners vs 28D churners separately.
Why it matters: If the recovery drop is structural (PayG customers just don't return), it will continue declining as PayG mix grows. If it's a campaign/effort issue, it's recoverable. The answer changes the forward projection materially — as you can see in the model below.
Required vs Current Renewal Rate — by PayG Plan Type
What renewal rate does each plan type need to generate no more churn per customer than a 28-day plan?
A 28D customer at 85% renewal contributes 3.75 lost per 100 customers/week (they hit R15 once every 4 weeks). For a 7-day customer hitting R15 every week, matching that requires 96.25% renewal. For a 1-day customer hitting R15 daily — 99.5%. No PayG plan currently meets its break-even threshold.
